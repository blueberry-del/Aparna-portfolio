I'm a designer and illustrator whose practice is rooted in understanding the ways in which knowledge and cultural hegemony manifest. Knowledge and culture that was never lost, only kept from being remembered, learned, shared and passed down. My work is shaped by a belief in the Pluriverse: that there are many valid ways of knowing, being and living, and that design has a responsibility to understand and learn from them rather than extract from or flatten them.

Children are also central to this practice. They are not just the future of our communities — they are already part of them, bringing ways of seeing and believing that adults have often forgotten how to access. Designing with and for children is, for me, inseparable from designing for a more honest world.

My work spans design research, ergonomics, community learning and unlearning. What holds it together is seeing design as a form of inquiry that takes seriously the knowledge which was never given the title of knowledge.
